UPSSSC Cane Supervisor Recruitment 2026: Official Notification & Complete Guide

Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Services Selection Commission (UPSSSC) has finally released the highly anticipated recruitment notification for the post of Cane Supervisor (Ganna Paryavekshak) for the year 2026. This recruitment drive is specifically targeted at candidates holding a Bachelor's Degree in Agriculture and possessing a valid UPSSSC PET (Preliminary Eligibility Test) scorecard. Read below for an exhaustive guide on eligibility, syllabus, and online application procedure.

Ensure that you complete the registration and fee payment within the stipulated timeline. The UPSSSC server experiences heavy loads during the final days, so applying early is highly recommended.

Application Fee Details

Unlike other competitive exams that have hefty application fees, UPSSSC charges a nominal processing fee for the initial Mains registration based on your PET score.

  • General / OBC / EWS: ₹25/-
  • SC / ST / PH: ₹25/-
  • Note: This ₹25 fee is strictly an online processing fee. If you are shortlisted for the Mains Exam, a separate substantial exam fee will be required later.
  • Payment Mode: Payment can be made exclusively via SBI e-Challan or strictly through Online modes (Debit Card, Credit Card, Internet Banking, UPI).

Eligibility Criteria & Age Limit

The Cane Supervisor post demands specific educational qualifications related to Agriculture. Please review the criteria below before spending time on the application.

  • Minimum Age: 21 Years
  • Maximum Age: 40 Years (Age calculated as per UP Govt Rules)
  • Relaxation: Extra age relaxation is provided for UP domicile candidates belonging to SC/ST/OBC categories.

Educational Qualifications (Mandatory)

  1. UPSSSC PET Scorecard: Candidates must possess a valid UPSSSC Preliminary Eligibility Test (PET) 2025/2026 scorecard with a positive (non-zero/non-negative) normalized score.
  2. Degree: A Bachelor’s Degree in Agriculture (B.Sc Ag) or equivalent from a recognized University in India.
  3. CCC Certificate: A CCC Computer Certificate from DOEACC/NIELIT is mandatory at the time of application.

Selection Process

The recruitment for UPSSSC Cane Supervisor is completely transparent and merit-based, conducted over two distinct stages:

  1. Shortlisting based on PET Score: Candidates who apply will be shortlisted for the Mains Examination strictly based on their percentile score in the UPSSSC PET. Usually, candidates numbering 15 times the total vacancies are called for the Mains.
  2. Mains Written Examination: The main objective-type exam will test your domain knowledge in Agriculture, UP Special Knowledge, and General Computer awareness.
  3. Document Verification: Qualifying candidates will undergo a rigorous verification of their B.Sc Ag degree, CCC certificate, and caste certificates. (No interview phase for Group C posts).

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Apply Online

Follow these meticulously detailed instructions to successfully submit your UPSSSC Cane Supervisor application:

  • Step 1: Navigate to the official UPSSSC website (upsssc.gov.in) and click on the "Live Advertisements" segment.
  • Step 2: Authentication: You have two choices for login:
    • Through Personal Details: Enter your PET Registration Number, DOB, Gender, Domicile, and Category.
    • Through OTP: Enter your PET Registration Number and the OTP received on your registered mobile number.
  • Step 3: Fill Details: Once authenticated, your basic details will be auto-filled from your PET profile. Enter the specific details for the Cane Supervisor post (e.g., your B.Sc Ag marks, CCC certificate issue date, and roll number).
  • Step 4: Upload Documents: Verify your photograph and signature (pre-loaded from PET). You do not need to upload them again unless specified.
  • Step 5: Fee Payment: Proceed to the payment gateway to pay the ₹25 processing fee.
  • Step 6: Final Submission: Click "Final Submit" and generate the application PDF. Keep this safe! You will need the Registration Number to download your Admit Card.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. Is the CCC Certificate absolutely mandatory?

A: Yes. Without a valid CCC certificate issued by NIELIT (formerly DOEACC) before the last date of application, you will be disqualified during Document Verification.

Q2. I scored only 50 marks in PET. Can I apply?

A: Yes, anyone with a positive score can apply and pay the ₹25 fee. However, whether you are called for the Mains exam depends entirely on the cutoff declared later by UPSSSC.

Q3. Can candidates from other states (like Bihar, MP) apply?

A: Yes, candidates from other states can apply provided they have the UPSSSC PET scorecard. However, they will be treated as Unreserved (General) category candidates and will not get any reservation benefits.
